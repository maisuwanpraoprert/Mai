Theory & Equipment

Theory

1

Gamma Interaction

Photoelectric Effect

Low-energy photon is fully absorbed by an atom, ejecting an inner-shell electron and disappearing.

Compton Scattering

Medium-energy photon hits an electron, losing part of its energy and scattering in a new direction.

Pair Production

High-energy photon (≥ 1.022 MeV) hits a nucleus, converting into an electron–positron pair.

2

Gamma Transmission

As gamma rays pass through the material, they are attenuated depending on the density and thickness of the object. The initial intensity is denoted as I₀, while the transmitted intensity is I. By measuring this attenuation, a density profile of the object can be obtained.

3

Distillation Column

Reboiler

Provides heat to the liquid at the bottom of the column and generates vapor that flows upward into the distillation column.

Condenser

Condenses vapor into liquid at the top of the column.

Tray

Provides contact surface between liquid and vapor to enhance mass transfer efficiency.

4

Counting System

Radiation Source (Am-241)

  • Provides stable 59.5 keV gamma rays to penetrate column walls and trays.
  • Signal intensity decreases based on the density and obstructions encountered.
  • Ideal energy level for high-resolution imaging in lab-scale models.

Detector (NaI Scintillator)

  • Captures residual gamma rays via the NaI(Tl) crystal.
  • Converts radiation into light pulses amplified by a PMT.
  • Provides precise count rates to map the internal density profile.

Gamma rays from the Am-241 source interact with the NaI(Tl) scintillator, producing light pulses that are processed by a Ludlum 2200 model. The ESP32 microcontroller captures and transmits this data to a live web dashboard, automatically plotting the density profile relative to the measurement height.
